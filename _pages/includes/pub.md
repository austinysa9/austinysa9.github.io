[Scalable Jet Swimmer Driven by Pulsatile Artificial Muscles and Soft Chamber Buckling](https://doi.org/10.1002/adma.202503777) \\
Wenhui Chen, **Shengan Yang**, Chi Zhu, Yitao Cheng, Yutong Shi, Chunpeng Yu, Ke Liu
- Knotted Artificial Muscles (KAMs), constructed from braided liquid crystal elastomer (LCE) fibers with conductive coatings and waterproof encapsulation, deliver 1.6× greater underwater strain, exceed 5000 actuation cycles in durability, and achieve energy densities over 100 J/kg—surpassing the performance of biological muscle.
- An integrated smart structural design amplifies actuation speed, enabling pulsatile underwater propulsion that exceeds the intrinsic limitations of the muscle materials alone.
- A simple yet responsive rudder facilitates guided locomotion via infrared-controlled directional steering.
- The system is inherently scalable, maintaining consistent performance across a range of sizes—from compact fast swimmers to larger long-range untethered robots.
